We, the members of the Historical Society of Nigeria, having firmly and solemnly resolved to form a non-governmental, non-partisan and non-profit organization, do hereby make and give to ourselves the following constitution:

The name of the organization shall be “Historical Society of Nigeria” hereafter referred to as the Society or HSN

The secretariat of the Society shall be at the Department of History, University of Ibadan, Ibadan, NIGERIA

The aims and objectives of Society shall be:

  1. To vigorously promote, support, strengthen and uphold the study of Nigerian History and Historical scholarship
  2. To through advocacy and strategic interventions, assiduously pursue the provisions of Section 4 (1) at all levels of education in
  3. To support individuals, institutions, government and non-governmental organizations in the establishment of all necessary structures that will enhance the study of History
  4. To promote an enduring sense of history and historical consciousness amongst the citizenry
  5. To conduct research into all aspects Nigerian History and promote the publication of such research endeavor.

  1. Membership shall be open to all persons genuinely interested and committed to the promotion of Historical scholarship in general and Nigerian History in particular.
  2. Any prospective member shall be admitted only upon sponsorship by two members of HSN and the Council shall after due deliberations decide whether or not to admit such proposed members.
  3. New members shall on admission pay the admission fee as in operation at the time of admission.
  4. New members shall subscribe to the oath of membership as prescribed in this constitution.
  5. With the exception of Life Members, membership of the Society shall be predicated on the payment of annual dues of the Society in any financial year.

  1. Student members-undergraduates through their affiliated student organizations. They shall be entitled to all priviledges of ordinary membership except that of voting or serving in any committee of the society.
  2. Associate members- council shall have the authority to admit, on recommendation, deserving members of the public and they shall be entitled to all priviledges of ordinary membership except that of voting or serving in any committee of the society.
  3. Ordinary members- Graduates of History and practitioners (i.e. teachers/Lecturers at all levels of education).
  4. Life Members- Any member who pays a stipulated bulk sum to be determined by Council. This category of membership is exempted from paying the Society’s annual dues.
  5. Fellows –These are distinguished Ordinary members of the society elevated to the status of Fellows by the Fellows in Council after such persons have
    • excelled in the promotion of Historical scholarship and demonstrated significant and persistent interest in the affairs of the Society.
    • engaged in the teaching of history in a tertiary institution and or practice other aspects of historical scholarship for at least 20 years and who must be an active member of the HSN.
    • must have consistently paid his membership fees for at least five years preceding his/her nomination. Such payments not made in arrears. In the case of a life member, such candidate must have been a Life Member for a period of at least three years.
    • Council shall have the authority to consider and admit into the Fellowship of the Society, after a prospective candidate; having satisfied all the constitutional provisions is recommended in writing by at least three members of Council who are Fellows, for consideration.
    • Any member, who after having become the President of Society, should be elevated to the status of a Fellow if not already one; at the next investiture of Fellows of the Society.
    • Associate Fellow- Council shall have the authority to admit, on recommendation of three Council members, deserving members of the public who have demonstrated outstanding interest in the promotion of Historical scholarship and/or preservation of Historical knowledge. Such persons shall be entitled to all priviledges of ordinary membership except that of voting or serving in any committee of the society.
  7. Corporate membersThese are corporate bodies who identify with the aims and objectives of the Society.

There shall be two types of Oaths:

  1. Oaths of intelligence and membership

    2. I ………………………………………, do solemnly swear/affirm that I will be faithful and bear true allegiance to the Historical Society of Nigeria and that I will preserve, protect and defend the constitution of Historical Society of Nigeria. So help me God.

  2. Oath of Office

    I ………………………………………, do solemnly swear/affirm that I will be faithful and bear true allegiance to the Historical Society of Nigeria and that as …………………………………………… of the Historical Society of Nigeria. I will discharge my duty to the best of my ability faithfully and for Historical Society of Nigeria. So help me God.

  1. member of HSN shall have the right to:
    • Attend all general meetings
    • Express his views as to the method(s) in which he/she feels the Society should be run.
    • Vote and be voted for in line with the provisions of this constitution and other bye laws and regulations as determined by Council/ Electoral body.
    • Receive the newsletter, discounts on any Society-sponsored publications, and reduced registration fee for the Society's annual conference.
  2. member shall have the responsibility to:
    • Fully exercise his right in good faith as a member of HSN.
    • Loyally and humbly support all activities of the HSN.
    • Attend all meetings to which he is constitutionally invited.
    • Attend all conferences of the Society.
    • Subscribe to all publications of the Society.
  1. HSN shall have a Board of Trustees consisting of SEVEN members
  2. Such trustees shall be known as THE INCORPORARATED TRUSTEES OF HISTORICAL SOCIETY OF NIGERIA and shall hereinafter be referred to as "Trustees".
  3. APPOINTMENT: The trustees of HSN for the purpose of the part "Appointment" of Companies and Allied Matters Act of 1990 shall be elected at a Council meeting of HSN.
  4. OFFICERS:

    The Board of Trustees shall have;

    • A Chairperson
    • A Vice-chairperson
    • A Secretary
    • All the above officers shall be elected from and by members of the Board.
  5. QUALIFICATION

    A person shall not be qualified to be appointed as a trustee if:

    • He is an infant.
    • He is a person of unsound mind having been so found by a court.
    • He is an undischarged bankrupt
    • He has been convicted of an offence involving fraud or dishonesty within five years of his proposed appointment.
    • He shall in addition to paragraphs (a) to (d) of Section 9(5) be committed to the aims and objectives of HSN.
  6. DUTIES
    • It shall be basically to advise council on all issues brought before it.
  7. TENURE

    The Trustees may hold office for five years in the first instance and another five years term, but shall cease to hold office if he :-

    • Resigns his office
    • Ceases to be a member of the Society
    • Becomes insane
    • Is officially declared a bankrupt
    • Convicted of a criminal offence involving dishonesty by a court of competent jurisdiction.
    • Is recommended for removal from office by 2/3rd majority votes of members present at any general meeting of Society.
    • ceases to reside in Nigeria.
    • Have served for a total of ten years on the board
  1. The Chairperson of the Board of trustees shall call and preside over all meetings of the Board.
  2. The vice-chairperson shall preside over meetings of the Board of Trustees in the absence of the chairperson.
  3. The Secretary shall communicate the decision of the Board of Trustees to the Council for the implementation/execution.
